3 # \mainpage Introduction
4 # This project is to provide controller software (server and GUI) for the instrument described below.
5 # It is being undertaken by "The Random Factory" (www.randomfactory.com) located in Tucson AZ.
6 #
7 # This effort is funded under NASA Grant and Cooperative agreement #NNX14AR61A
8 #
9 # Principal Investigator : Steve B. Howell, NASA Ames Research Center, Senior Research Scientist
10 #
11 # The NN-EXPLORE program provides about 50 percent of the observing time on the Kitt Peak WIYN (Wisconsin-Indiana-Yale-NOAO) 3.5-meter
12 # telescope to the exoplanet community.
13 # WIYN’s suite of instruments include HYDRA, a multi-fiber medium to high-resolution bench #spectrograph,
14 # WHIRC, a near-IR imager, ODI, an optical wide-field optical imager, and for over a decade,
15 # a visiting speckle camera #called DSSI (Horch et al. 2009).
16 #
17 # The telescope’s instruments also include several integral field units, bundles of optical fiber that
18 # feed light from the telescope to an instrument, in this case a spectrograph, that lives in an environmentally
19 # controlled room in the WIYN telescope basement.
20 #
21 # Beginning in 2017, thanks to funding support from the NASA Exoplanet program, DSSI has been replaced by a
22 # modern, more functional, community available observatory instrument named NESSI.
23 #
24 # NESSI, the NN-EXPLORE Exoplanet & Stellar Speckle Imager, was commissioned during the fall of 2016 \
25 # and is now available for community #use. Speckle imaging allows telescopes to achieve diffraction limited
26 # imaging performance—that is, collecting images with resolutions equal to that which would be possible if the atmosphere were removed. # The technique employs digital cameras capable of reading out frames at a very fast rate, effectively “freezing out”
27 # atmospheric seeing. The resulting speckles are correlated and combined in Fourier space to produce reconstructed
28 # images with resolutions at the diffraction limit of the telescope (see Howell et al., 2011).
29 # Achievable spatial resolutions at WIYN are 39 milliarcseconds (550 nanometers) and 64 milliarcseconds (880 nanometers).
